Opaque chips Recommended

Drop the transparency entirely. A solid bright-gold pill (dark text) for holidays, a solid red pill (white text) for closures. Opaque fills are background-independent, so they read identically on white and sapphire. Both pass WCAG AA. Most robust, slightly louder.

Color spine + white tag Strong

A small label tag sits on its own near-white backing, with a colored spine bar carrying the meaning. The white backing means the colored text always has contrast, even on the dark header. Reads like a tidy file tab; quieter than full chips.

Dot + adaptive label Cleanest

The label uses currentColor — dark on the white cell, white on the sapphire header — so contrast is automatic. A colored dot carries the holiday/closure distinction. The lightest-touch, most "calendar-native" look; the trade-off is the type/color signal is subtler.
