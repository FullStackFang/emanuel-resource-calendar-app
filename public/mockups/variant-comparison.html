What's the same across all three?
  • Always-visible chevron at 0.35 opacity (the resting affordance signal). On hover/focus it lifts to full opacity, translates 4px right, and shifts color to info-blue.
  • Hover background is a warm-stone tint (color-mix on neutral-100), not info-blue. Reads as "you're hovering me," not as "I'm selected."
  • Date underline is a 1px hairline that fades in on hover (info-500 color, 3px offset). Hyperlink convention.
  • Easing is cubic-bezier(0.32, 0.72, 0, 1) over 200ms — refined ease-out, no bounce.
  • Cancelled rows are visibly inert: 0.62 opacity, strikethrough date, no chevron, no hover/click.
  • Reduced motion: all transitions and translates suppressed under prefers-reduced-motion.
